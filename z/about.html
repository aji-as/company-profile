100% Direct Trade Partnerships
Chapter I

Honoring the
Source

Our journey begins thousands of miles away, on the mist-covered slopes of Ethiopia, Colombia, and Costa Rica. We don't just buy beans; we build relationships.

By partnering directly with small-lot farmers, we ensure that every hand involved in the process is treated with dignity and paid a premium wage. This ethical foundation creates a cup that tastes better because it is better.

  • check_circle Single Origin Selection
  • check_circle Sustainable Farming Practices
  • check_circle Seasonal Harvests Only
Chapter II

The Art of
Precision

Once the beans arrive at our roastery, science meets art. We roast in small batches to highlight the unique terroir of each region—preserving bright acidity, deep chocolates, and floral aromatics.

In the cafe, our baristas are craftsmen. Whether it's a V60 pour-over timed to the second or espresso pulled at the perfect pressure, we treat every cup as a masterpiece.

Small Batch Roasting
Q-Grade Certified Team
Chapter III

An Urban Sanctuary

We designed Kanara House to be the "third place"—a refuge between home and work. Inspired by Japandi aesthetics and biophilic design, our space is built to help you decompress.

spa

Mindful Atmosphere

Soft lighting, curated acoustic playlists, and abundant greenery create an environment that lowers cortisol and sparks creativity.

diversity_2

Community Hub

From morning cuppings to evening poetry slams, we host events that bring the neighborhood together in meaningful ways.

design_services

Intentional Design

Every chair, table, and cup is chosen for comfort and aesthetics, ensuring your physical experience matches the quality of the coffee.

"We didn't just want to serve coffee; we wanted to serve peace of mind."

A Note from Elena

"When I started Kanara House, I was exhausted by the noise of the city. I wanted a place that felt like a deep breath. Over the years, we've grown, but that core intention remains. Every bean we roast and every cup we pour is an invitation for you to pause, reflect, and enjoy the moment."

Founder & Head Roaster
